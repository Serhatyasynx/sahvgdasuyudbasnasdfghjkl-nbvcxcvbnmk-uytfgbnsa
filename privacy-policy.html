    • ​Log files: A log file is a file that records events that occur in connection with your use of the Service, such as your service usage data.
    • ​Cookies: A cookie is a small data file stored on your device that acts as a unique tag to identify your browser. We use two types of cookies: session cookies and persistent cookies. Persistent cookies help you personalize your experience, remember your preferences, and support security features. Additionally, persistent cookies allow you to turn the Service both on and off. Persistent cookies can stay on your device for a long time and can usually be controlled by your browser settings.

There are several types of pixels, including image pixels (small graphic images) and JavaScript pixels (containing JavaScript code). When you access a website, video, email, or ad that contains a pixel, the pixel may allow us or a third party to set or read cookies in your browser. Pixels are used with cookies to track activity by a particular browser on a particular device. We may add pixels from third parties that allow us to track our conversions, turn you both on and off the Service, and provide additional functionality such as the ability to link our Service to your social media account.
    • ​Device Fingerprint: Device fingerprinting is the process of analyzing and combining sets of information elements such as JavaScript objects and installed fonts from your device's browser to create a 'fingerprint' of your device and uniquely identify your device and applications.
    • ​Application Technologies: There are various tracking technologies that can be incorporated into our apps and they are not like cookie-based browsers and can not be controlled by browser settings. For example, our apps may contain third-party SDKs, which are code that sends information about your usage to a server and is actually the app version of a pixel. These SDKs allow us to track our conversions, bring you both on and off the Service, as well as provide additional functionality such as connecting our Service to your social media account.
    • ​Positioning Technologies: GPS, WiFi, Bluetooth, and other location-aware technologies may be used to collect precise location data when you enable location-based services on your device. Location data may be used for purposes such as verifying your device's location and serving or restricting relevant content and advertisements based on that location.
